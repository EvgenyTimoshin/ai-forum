Agentic Workflow

A system where an LLM is empowered to decide its own control flow to solve complex problems. This often involves breaking tasks into sequential, manageable steps and collaborating with other specialized AI agents.

ReAct Framework

Stands for "Reason and Act." It's a framework where an agent first verbalizes its reasoning process or "thought" about a problem, and then chooses an action (like using a tool) based on that reasoning.

Human-in-the-Loop

A process where key decisions or actions made by an AI agent must be approved by a human before execution. This is a common pattern for ensuring safety and reliability in high-stakes applications.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

A technique where an LLM's knowledge is supplemented by retrieving relevant information from an external knowledge base before generating a response. This helps to ground the model's output in factual, up-to-date information.
